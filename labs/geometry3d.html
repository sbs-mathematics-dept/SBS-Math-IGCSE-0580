3D Geometry

Draw accurate nets to scale on a 1 cm² grid, look up faces / edges / vertices, and calculate volume and surface area with the working shown.

Solid
Example:

Each grid square = 1 cm. The net is drawn to scale — the area of the net equals the total surface area of the solid.

Euler's formula for solids with flat faces: Faces + Vertices − Edges = 2. (It does not apply to curved solids like the cylinder, cone or sphere.)
